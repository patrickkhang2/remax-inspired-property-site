The Journey

Turned 22 and did Ironman 70.3 — something I’ve always wanted to achieve.

With only 3 months to train after my overseas trip, it became one of the hardest challenges I’ve ever taken on.

Early mornings. Late nights. Training 5–6 days a week.

Over time, it took a toll — a limping knee, a twisted ankle, and long, lonely sessions filled with doubt and low motivation.

There were moments where no one around you truly understands what you're doing — or why you’d willingly go through this kind of suffering alone.

But Ironman forces you to find your why. It pushes you to discover your values — and what really carries you through when things get tough.

After a 1.9km swim, 90km bike, and 21.1km run — I crossed the finish line and found my answers.

It wasn’t the time I had hoped for, but I’ve been hardened by pain and humbled by the experience.

I trained.
I broke.
I became.
