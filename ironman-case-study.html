I've finally ticked off something I've always wanted to do.

Having only 3 months to train after my overseas trip was definitely the hardest thing I've ever done. It meant a lot of early mornings and late nights, training 5–6 days a week.

Eventually at times, it started to take a toll on my body. A limping knee. A twisted ankle. And a lot of lonely moments filled with doubt and lack of motivation.

There were days when no one in your world really understands what you're doing, or why you'd put yourself through this kind of suffering alone.

— The hardest part

But Ironman forced me to discover my why, my values, and what truly carries you through when things get tough.

And after a 1.9km swim, 90km bike ride and 21.1km run, I've finally crossed the finish line and found my answers.

Although this wasn't the time I was hoping to achieve, I've been hardened by pain yet humbled by the experience.
